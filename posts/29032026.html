Week 13: midsummer thunderstorms

March 29, 2026

When it rained, it poured

Hello!

It rained other day. Not just any rain but with thunderstorm, lightning, heavy wind as well. The effect is visible, square meter plot turned greener and it felt a bit like monsoon the next day!

Yet, the number of bugs I see is still quite low compared the January winter. Summer really affects them.

My attention is still a bit helter-skelter, owing to life outside the square meter.

That said, a tree frog visited the plot and chilled around for a while. I am not too sure if the ID is correct though.

So, first quarter of the year is over. Here is the data:

The new bugs discovery as such has dramatically (more than 90%) reduced since the summer arrived.

But, the number of bugs (repeat observations) I see had reduced slightly but only by about 35% or so.

But, given these discoveries and observations are totally dependent on me and my ability to be present, I can not pin the changes in squarely on to the weather just yet.

’Til next week!
