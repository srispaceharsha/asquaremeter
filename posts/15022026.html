Week 7: It is complicated.

February 15, 2026

It is nice to see a bug that I hadn't seen in a while.

Hello!

Aphids. These tiny, plumpy bugs (~ 1 mm in length) are at the center of a dramatic relationship between various ants, ladybugs and wasps.

It took me a good one month to even notice these tiny moving things! Now I see the drama around them everyday.

So, the aphids drink the plant's sap. But the sap is too sugary for aphids (I mean, no wonder they look so round!). So they secrete the excess sugar as "honeydew". So far, so good.

But sugar is like money of the insect world and everyone wants in.

The carpenter (and other types) ants "farm" these aphids for the sweet, sweet nectar. They kinda massage the aphids to "help" them secrete the honeydew. They move the aphids to juicy parts of the plant and even defend them from predators.

I’ve seen carpenter ants climbing up and down the grass all day to tend their aphids.

Interestingly, I noticed these red ants (Weaver ants I guess?) doing similar rounds as well!

Clearly, this is a profitable business.

But then again, more opportunistic insects thought why take the trouble for farming them, taking care of them when you can simply eat them? That's what the aptly named aphid wasps do. The other day I saw this aphid hunter running up and down the grass to select the best aphid and flying away with it!

Likewise, the ladybugs munch on these aphids too. I've seen them gobbling on these aphids all day long only to be interrupted by, you guessed it, the carpenter ants. Their face-off was quite underwhelming but photographing it wasn't easy.

Such a quiet drama, all day (and night) long and all within the square meter.

’Til next week!
