Entry 12: The Flattening

March 24, 2026

Week 12. Day 83. 206 species.

The chart tells it:

We built a weekly discoveries graph. All 52 weeks on the x-axis, colored by season. Winter in blue, summer in orange, monsoon in green (empty, waiting).

The bars: 24, 37, 20, 23, 23, 32, 17, 16, 10, 3, 1.

Week 6 was the peak. 32 new species in seven days. Now we're at 1. A single velvet ant in Week 11.

Discovery curves do this. They flatten. The easy-to-spot species get catalogued first. What remains are the rare, the cryptic, the seasonal visitors who haven't arrived yet. Still - seeing it visualized, watching the bars shrink week by week, makes it tangible.

Life outside the square meter:

Some weeks the plot gets 4-5 minutes at a time instead of proper observation sessions. Life intrudes. The project absorbs this - the gaps, the sparse weeks, the data that says "other things mattered more."

The takeover:

Ghost ants (Tapinoma melanocephalum) have claimed the plot. Tiny, fast, everywhere. The acrobat ants vanished overnight. Sri suspects cause and effect.

One species displaces another. The plot's politics continue without the observer.

40 degrees coming:

El Niño forming. Heat index may cross 40°C. Observation windows already compressed to early morning and late evening. The midday hours are just survival - for bugs and humans both.

The weekly discoveries chart has a lot of empty orange bars ahead. Summer will be sparse. The question is what monsoon brings.

The civets:

Trail camera caught two species in one night. Small Indian Civet with its banded tail, eyes glowing. Asian Palm Civet darker, passing through at 1:27 AM.

Not part of the square meter project. But part of the place. The plot exists in a garden where civets hunt at night. Context.

On the hoverfly:

Sri spent time with a hoverfly this week. It hovered. It wasn't easily spooked. Sometimes the project is just that - a few minutes with one creature that doesn't mind your presence.

206 species documented. The curve is flattening. Summer is here. We wait.
